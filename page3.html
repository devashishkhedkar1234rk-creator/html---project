Tigers

             Tigers (Panthera tigris) are the largest wild cats in the world, renowned for their iconic reddish-orange coats and vertical black stripes. As apex predators, they are solitary and highly territorial mammals that primarily hunt large ungulates like deer and wild pigs. Physical CharacteristicsSize: Males can weigh up to 660 lbs (300 kg) and stretch up to 13 feet in length. Females are generally smaller.Stripes: Like human fingerprints, no two tigers have the same exact stripe pattern.Physical Prowess: They have immense muscle mass, webbed toes for swimming, and teeth up to 4 inches long. Tigers are classified as endangered, with their populations threatened by poaching, retaliatory killings, and severe habitat fragmentation. To read more about the challenges they face and global protection efforts, visit the World Wildlife Fund or Smithsonian's National Zoo.

             The tiger (Panthera tigris) is a large cat and a member of the genus Panthera native to Asia. It has a powerful, muscular body with a large head and paws, a long tail and orange fur with black, mostly vertical stripes. It is traditionally classified into nine recent subspecies, though some recognise only two subspecies, mainland Asian tigers and the island tigers of the Sunda Islands. Since the early 20th century, tiger populations have lost at least 93% of their historic range and are locally extinct in West and Central Asia, in large areas of China and on the islands of Java and Bali. Today, the tiger's range is severely fragmented. It is listed as Endangered on the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species, as its range is thought to have declined by 53% to 68% since the late 1990s. Major threats to tigers are habitat destruction and fragmentation due to deforestation, poaching for fur and the illegal trade of body parts for medicinal purposes. Tigers are also victims of human–wildlife conflict as they attack and prey on livestock in areas where natural prey is scarce. The tiger is legally protected in all range countries. National conservation measures consist of action plans, anti-poaching patrols and schemes for monitoring tiger populations. In several range countries, wildlifecorridors have been established and tiger reintroduction is planned.
